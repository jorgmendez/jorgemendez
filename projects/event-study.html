Abstract

I evaluate market reaction to Microsoft‚Äôs $68.7B acquisition of Activision Blizzard announced on Jan 18, 2022. Using an estimation window to fit expected returns and an event window to measure impacts, I compute abnormal returns (AR) and cumulative abnormal returns (CAR). A two-sample t-test shows a significant mean shift; CAPM and a sector-adjusted multifactor model confirm significance. CAR increases ‚âà +29% post-announcement.

Data

  • Sources: Yahoo Finance daily prices for ATVI and S&P 500.
  • Series used: close prices and computed daily log/percent returns.
  • Preprocessing: cleaned dates, aligned trading days, computed returns in R/Excel.
Financial returns are not perfectly normal, but with n‚âà144 the t-test is robust; CLT justifies inference on mean differences.

Methodology

Event study steps: (1) Fit expected returns on the estimation window; (2) Compute AR = RATVI ‚àí E[RATVI] on event days; (3) Accumulate to CAR; (4) Test significance.

  • t-test: two-sample mean comparison (estimation vs event window returns).
  • CAPM: \( R_{ATVI,t} - R_{f,t} = \alpha + \beta (R_{M,t} - R_{f,t}) + \varepsilon_t \)
  • Multifactor: CAPM + sector index (ESPO) to capture gaming/esports exposure.
